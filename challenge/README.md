# Judging Criteria

Submissions will be evaluated across five dimensions:

1. **Technical Soundness** — Are the methods correct, internally consistent, and grounded in reasonable assumptions?
2. **Depth of Analysis** — Does the work go beyond surface-level results to examine behavior, limitations, and tradeoffs?
3. **Originality** — Does the submission reflect independent thinking or creative adaptation, rather than straightforward replication?
4. **Impact and Relevance** — Does the solution meaningfully address the core problem and articulate why it matters?
5. **Clarity and Organization** — Is the work (notebooks, slides, etc.) easy to follow, well-structured, and clearly communicated?

Strong submissions may produce novel, publishable research of direct relevance to Alice & Bob. Be proud of your work!

# Submission

Submissions have two components:

### DoraHacks Submissions

In DoraHacks, upload your repository to submit.

- Create a folder named `team-<name>/` at the root of the repository (e.g., `team-cats/`).
- **Do not modify or delete** the `challenge/` folder.
- Your team folder should contain all submission materials: code, papers, slides, a README, etc.

```
challenge/         ← no modifications
team-<your_team_name>/
├── README.md      ← required; describe your submission contents
├── notebook.ipynb ← optional example
├── slides.pdf     ← optional (or link in README)
└── paper.pdf      ← optional
```

A clear `team-<name>/README.md` is required and will be the first thing reviewers read.
